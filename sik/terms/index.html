Data collection and processing


SIK collects data from your phone to provide you with context-aware and dynamic music recommendations with your feedback in your everyday life. Our primary goals in collecting information are to provide and improve our Services, to administer your use of the Services, and to enable you to enjoy and easily navigate our Services. When you install, run or use our services we collect:


  • Personally Identifiable Information. When you engage in certain actions on the Services, including but not limited to creating an account, SIK collects certain information that can be used to identify you, such as your name, email address, birth year, access location, and profile image (“Personally Identifiable Information” or “PII”).


  • Location and Activity Data. Our system starts to collect location and motion sensor data from sources once you install the App and consent to the App’s tracking your location and activity data. We collect GPS, Wi-Fi, cell towers, Accelerometer samples, Wi-Fi network IDs, activity data, headphone and Bluetooth connections. You may choose to stop our collection of location data through the Settings that we provide in the App, or by removing the App from your phone. We track your movement patterns and how long you are stationary at specific locations. We analyze these patterns to generate statistics about activities and use them to provide and improve real-time Services. We do not share your individual location or activity information with third parties, without your explicit consent. We may, however, use anonymized, aggregated data to improve Human as a whole. It helps us optimize our activity detection and measure the overall performance of the App. We might also use aggregated stats, which cannot be tied to individual users, to help you compare yourself to others.


  • Information from your device. This includes information about your operating system, device identifier, carrier, language, battery performance, wi-fi or other network connections, or other data that you permit the App to access on your device including through permissions on your device (e.g. Google Play on Android).


  • Information from your Third Party Accounts. When you connect your use of the Services with one of your Third Party Accounts, such as Spotify or Facebook, you shall have to provide us with your username (or userID), gender, number of followers, saved songs, playlists, and top tracks information so that your identity can be authenticated by the Third Party Account. When the authentication is complete, we’ll be able to link your use of the Services with the Third Party Account and provided real-time recommendation of songs based on your information, share your User Content with such Third Party Account according to the selections you make via the Services. We don’t receive or store passwords for any of your Third Party Accounts.


  • Communications with us. If you communicate with us, we collect the information and content you provide to us, including personally identifying information such as your name, email or other contact information. You can provide anonymous feedback by using an email account that does not reveal your identity.


How we use information


We process and use the information we collect about you in a variety of ways. The primary purpose is to provide better Services by tracking how and where you move in your everyday life. We upload PII from your device over a secured connection to our servers in order to analyze the data and identify your activities, routes and places. Results are sent back to your phone over a secured connection so that you can view and interact with them, and they are stored on our servers. If you indicate whether like or dislike a song while using our Service, we also collect and store that information to provide better a recommendation of songs during a specific situation.


Other ways we use your data include to develop aggregated analyses and reports that help us improve our algorithms, measure and understand how our Services are used, and to develop new products, services and features such as by recognizing popular activities, routes, areas and places. We also use PIIto communicate with you, such as by sending you notifications and service-related messages, or by responding to your requests and questions.
